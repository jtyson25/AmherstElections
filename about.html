About

This initiative aims to align Amherst's municipal elections with federal and local cycles. By switching to "on-cycle" elections, Amherst will improve democracy and cut down on unnecessary costs. If successfully passed, Amherst would be the first Massachusetts city to make such a change, setting the pace for the rest of the state.

FAQ

How do on-cycle elections improve voter turnout?

By consolidating local races with high-visibility national and state elections, on-cycle elections benefit from greater public awareness and mobilization. Unsurprisingly, too, when voters have fewer elections to pay attention to, they suffer less from "voter fatigue" and participate more.

How do on-cycle elections increase representation?

In off-cycle elections, the resources, time, and information required to vote can be prohibitive. As a result, studies have shown that voter bases in odd-year elections are whiter, richer, higher-educated, and older than in even-years. Aligning elections improves representation and increases access.
